    Please provide a brief description of your training and awards.

    My mum and sister have been a career-long mentor. I have been drawing and coloring since my kindergarten with my elder sister who taught me all the basics. I have taken art classes in the school as part of the curriculum and I have also attended numerous arts enrichment programs organized by community members and institutes during the summers in my primary and secondary schooling years.

    I have received formal education and training in various forms of visual arts which includes but are not limited to drawing, painting, crafting, ceramics, clay modelling, designing, bamboo arts, glass painting, calligraphy, etc. I like to portray my tradition and society with my artistic nature and creative intelligence.

    Unfortunately, the field of arts is not considered as competitive as engineering and medical sciences according to the social conventions in India. It happens to be one of the reasons for my transition to engineering but I continue to record my stories in the form of freehand drawings to achieve my goal of being an artist.

    Lord Ganesha

    Description: This potrait of lord Ganesha was made as a part of canvas painting by me for a presentation in my school. The artwork symbolises the Hindu god, who is worshipped before all the other hindu gods, he is an elephant-headed god, son of lord Shiva and Parvati. Ganesha is a very popular god in hinduism and is one of the most worshipped. Hindu tradition states that ganesha is a god of wisdom, success and goodluck.

    Here I have used canvas and poster colours to make this painting of our divine power, in a belief that the qualities that he represents gets kindled within me.

    Here GANA: signifies the ultimate truth that this existing world is nothing but a collection of molecule form called as "GANA".
    ELEPHANT HEAD: signifies authority, endurance strength and courage.
    BIG BELLY: signifies generosity and total acceptance.
    SINGLE TUSK: signifies one- pointedness, i.e our focus towards our goal.
    UPRAISED HAND: depicts protection.
    LOWERED HAND: signifies endless giving and also symbolises the fact that we will all dissolve into the earth one day.

    Overall I chose to paint this ,to express divinity in terms of symbols rather than words, since words change over by time but symbols remains unchanged.
