    POSTER COLOR PAINTING OF SIDDHIVINAYAK

    Lord Ganesha is the most worshipped god in Hindu mythology. He is considered to be the god of wisdom, success and good luck.

    A canvas was flashed with poster colors to make this divine art. The elephant-headed lord was drawn then painted using different brown shades for the body and blue for the loincloth; shades of red and brown pink were used to decorate the ornaments. The upper half of the background was given yellow, red and white strokes, and the lower half painted as peacock blue. The appropriate choices of colors put breath to the portrait.

    As the lord is praised with great enthusiasm during the ten-day festival of “Ganeshotsav” and its elephant head signifies authority, endurance, strength and courage, it was a part of this festival. It symbolizes that the existing world is nothing but a collection of molecule form called “GANA.” This portrait expresses divinity in terms of symbols rather than words, since words change over time, but symbols remain unchanged.

    CERAMIC POT DESIGN FOR WORLD ENVIRNOMENT DAY

    An earthen pot is used all over the Indian sub-continent as a water cooler. Decoration of earthen pots is an art form in India. Decorated pots act as a showpiece to decorate houses. I created this art form as part of a competition in school on 'World Environment Day' to showcase our planet's importance and the various environmental challenges we face.

    The art form was created by embossing the designs on the earthen pot using a mixture of ceramic. After the ceramic was dried and firm, I colored it with poster paints and then painted a layer of oil paint to give it a glossy appearance. The 1st design depicts a mother who is a source of life to the child, just like a drop of water, which is the source of life to all living forms to highlight the importance of water. The 2nd design depicts a tree holding water and shielding the earth here depicted by the earthen pot with its leaves, emphasizing how trees are essential for our planet's survival.

    WARLI PAINTING

    Warli painting is a tribal art from the North Sahayadri Ranges of India. It is also one of the finest examples of the folk style of painting from my native place. This form of art uses basic geometric shapes, such as circles, squares, triangles, etc. The shapes are symbolic of elements of nature, such as a circle representing the moon or the sun and triangles representing mountains or trees. This painting is inspired by the works of Jivya Soma Mashe, who is a pioneer of the Warli art form. The theme of this painting is around the celebration of the festival of lights ‘Diwali’. People in the painting can be seen dancing around a fire, burning crackers, cooking delicious meals in their houses. Traditionally these paintings are created on the inside walls of the village using white paint. My art form was created on black paper using white chalk. A lot of thought has gone into symmetric alignment of objects, the depiction of activities, designing the objects to create a beautiful masterpiece.

    NOTAN PAINTING OF TAJ MAHAL

    The Taj Mahal is a beautiful ivory-white mausoleum on the southern bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra. It is one of the seven wonders of the world. It is a symbol of love since Mughal emperor Shah Jahan built it to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal; This sketch was drawn as an inspiration after visiting the site.

    The drawing follows the Japanese concept of Notan, where we draw the underlying structure using white and black shades. Where white represents the light and black the shadows. The image was first sketched using a pencil, then painted using black and white poster colors and finally, the finishing touch highlighting was done using a black marker. There is adequate attention to details given in this painting to draw the monument's murals and patterns, creating a reflection in the spring and creating shadows wherever necessary.

    ALPANA

    The word "Rangoli" is derived from the Sanskrit word "Rangavalli," it signifies a creative expression of art using colors. Rangoli is an art form originating in the Indian subcontinent. Patterns are created on the floor or the ground using colored rice, colored sand, quartz powder, flower petals, and colored rocks. The purpose of drawing a rangoli is to induce a feeling of strength, generosity and to bring good luck to one's family.

    I drew this piece of art during Diwali. It is a 'Sanskar Bharti' rangoli art form. Colored sand in white, blue, green, orange, pink, red and yellow was used to create this beautiful carpet design. The symbol of 'Swastika' in the middle symbolizes the sun, prosperity and good luck. This art form requires acute precision and a steady hand while creating white strokes. It also requires a good understanding of color schemes and color combinations.

    MURAL ART OF ASHTAVINAYAKA

    Ganesha was known as the “Lord of the people (ganas).” He took eight forms to defeat eight weaknesses in human beings: envy, arrogance, delusion, greed, anger, desire, attachment and self-infatuation. This painting was created as a tribute to Ganesha and his eight forms as they inspire me to defeat any weaknesses of character and any challenges that come my way.

    This art form took me two months to create and has brought me a lot of appreciation from everyone. I started by drawing the layout on a cardboard canvas, and then I started making the embossing guided by the layout using the ceramic mixture. Consistency of ceramic mixture and patience is key to this process. Then once the embossing was done, I painted the artwork using poster colors. Painting on embossing requires a meticulous effort as you have to paint different shades of the same color for the shadow and light parts. For the final touch, I painted it with oil paints to give it a glossy appearance.
