    I was inspired to consider a gap year after I came across a blog written by previous Director of Admissions at Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

    Life, college, career — it’s not a race.

    Inspired by the blog written by Sir Matt, I went forward to live my dream to explore my country and pursue my academic interests at national institutes located in different regions of my country.




    Hyderabad | Guwahati | Indore | Roorkee | Delhi | Ahmedabad | Gandhinagar | Mumbai | Belgaum | Banglore | Madras
    IIT Guwahati | IIT Indore | IIT Roorkee | IIT Hyderabad | IIT Delhi | IIT Bombay | IIT Madras

    I moved to Hyderabad (Telangana) to live with my cousins and to pursue an advanced course on computer-aided design at Canter CADD India. I spent quality time living with my family and also learned to cook a variety of North and South Indian cuisine. I learned to travel alone and manage daily chores. It was my first time exploring a Tier I city all on my own.

    I represented the annual techno-management and cultural festival of IIT Guwahati (Techniche), IIT Delhi (Rendezvous), and IIM Indore (IRIS) as a technical representative and student ambassador. I also volunteered for Machine Learning India and Maker Faire Hyderabad. It was a valuable experience as I gained leadership qualities and built my network.

    I was selected nationally to attend the Biotech Innovation Ignition School for which I traveled to a village called Grambharti Amrapur (Gujrat) to research at SRISTI Labs. I studied mastitis in dairy cattle and implemented novel low-cost paper-based bio-sensing system. I enjoyed village life, especially real Indian flavors.



    Throughout the year 2019, I followed the research work of Dr. Chris Harrison (Carnegie Mellon University) and implemented advanced human interface devices. I traveled to Belgaum and Bangalore (Karnataka) to participate in the Indian Science and Engineering Fair, where I won First Award at the regional fair and Third award at the national fair.







    The pandemic ruined my plans for 2020 though my gap experience has been phenomenal and breathtaking so far. In the present, I continue to follow my academic interests studying online on NPTEL, MIT OCW, Coursera, and edX.
