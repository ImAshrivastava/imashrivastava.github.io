    Types of Application & the Difference
    Restrictive Early Action (REA) Early Decision (ED) Early Action (EA)
    Under Restrictive Early Action, you may not apply to any other schools in the early rounds. Applicants are expected to sign a statement that they agree to file only one early application. REA is non-binding and you aren’t obligated to attend if accepted.

    		Unlike EA and REA, Early Decision is a binding early round application. If you are accepted Early Decision, you must withdraw any applications to other schools and enroll to the one to which you applied ED.. Some schools allow you to apply early to other programs, as long as they are not binding and restrictive, while others ask that you submit only one early application.

    Application Portals
    Common Application Coailition Application MIT Appliaction UC Appliaction
    Apply via Common App, most universities accept it.
    • Applications: 20 only
    • Activites: 10 allowed
      (limited description)
    • Honors & Awards: 5 allowed (limited description)
    • Fee Waiver: International Applicants eligible

    (Recommended)     		Alternatively, universities also accept Coalition App.
    • Applications: No limit
    • Activities: 8 allowed
      (slightly more description)
    • Honors & Awards: 5 allowed
      (slightly more description)
    • Fee Waiver: International Applicants eligible
