    What have I been doing since I left school?

    Matt McGann, previous Director of Admissions at MIT, said in his 2011 blog, “No one ever regrets having taken a gap year, but plenty of people regret not having taken one.” I am one among those who haven’t regretted taking a gap year.

    The distinct and glorious National Institutes of India have always been intriguing for me which led to my consideration of taking time off to seek guidance from scientists and students who were living my dream life.

    In pursuit of this, I first moved to Hyderabad (Telangana) to pursue an advanced course on computer-aided design at Canter CADD India. I learned to travel alone and manage daily chores. It was my first time exploring a Tier I city all on my own.

    Further I traveled to IIT Guwahati (Assam), IIT Indore (Madhya Pradesh), IIT Roorkee (Uttarakhand), IIT Delhi, IIT Hyderabad, IIT Bombay (Maharashtra), and IIT Madras (Tamil Nadu) consecutively to witness the largest engineering festivals in India. I represented the annual techno-management and cultural festival of IIT Guwahati (Techniche), IIT Delhi (Rendezvous), and IIM Indore (IRIS) as a technical representative and ambassador on behalf of the event administration committee. I also volunteered for Machine Learning India and Maker Faire Hyderabad. It was a valuable experience as I gained leadership qualities and built my network.

    I made new friends and enjoyed hostel life while pursuing introductory courses on robotics, machine learning, web development, cloud computing, automation design, internet of things, ethical hacking, and MATLAB.

    I was selected nationally to attend the Biotech Innovation Ignition School by Society for Research and Initiative for Sustainable Technologies and Institutions for which I travelled to a village called Grambharti Amrapur (Gujrat) to research at SRISTI Labs. I studied mastitis in dairy cattle and implemented a novel low-cost paper-based bio-sensing system. I enjoyed village life, especially real Indian flavors.

    Throughout the year 2019, I followed the research work of Dr. Chris Harrison (CMU, chrisharrison.net) and implemented advanced human interface devices. I travelled to Belgaum and Bangalore (Karnataka) to participate in the Indian Science and Engineering Fair, where I won First Award at the Regional Fair and Third award at the National Fair.

    The pandemic ruined my plans for 2020 though my gap experience has been phenomenal and breath taking so far. At present, I continue to follow my academic interests studying online on NPTEL, MIT OCW, Coursera, and edX.

    What historical moment or event do you wish you could have witnessed?

    On November 21, 1963, India successfully launched the subcontinent’s first sounding rocket developed in a cattle shed from a launchpad made in a field of coconut groves. I wish I could have witnessed this event which gave rise to the Indian Space Research Organisation.

    Something I do for the pleasure of it.

    I love to spend time with my friends and family trying activities with a little twist and blend of flavors. Outside the cubicle rooms and away from the technological world, I spend most of my time exploring different cuisines offered by local food shops and unique food start-ups. I have an immense interest in culinary arts.

    Every week as I learn more about the food that I taste with my friends and family, I come up with a well-organized plan to spend the weekend learning how to cook my favorite dishes. The range of classic and unique recipes lure me to put together the mix of spices and ingredients to prepare mouth-watering, enticing Indian dishes. As I compulsively chop, stir, roast, and cook, I experiment and satisfy my urge to be creative and imaginative.

    The buzz of Bollywood music soothes my mind as I match with the rhythm to hop, jump, and dance while artistically garnishing the delicious food. The spicy food's aroma welcomes my family, including my little stray animals, to enjoy the precious moments of our lives.

    Cooking with love provides food for the soul. Cooking is love made edible. I serve my love to my friends and family, and we take a moment to be grateful for our lives. As I sit with them to relish the food, I cherish that time, expressing how much I love being with them. I love cooking simply for the pleasure and happiness it gives me.
