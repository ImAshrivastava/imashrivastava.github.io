Raman Science Centre and Planetarium Appreciation Award at Raman Festival of Innovation.
2018
The Raman Festival of Innovation is an annual science and technology event organized by the Raman Science Centre & Planetarium in Nagpur, under the aegis of the National Council of Science Museums (NCSM), Ministry of Culture, Government of India. This festival serves as a platform for students, innovators, and technologists to showcase their creative projects and working models across various disciplines of science and engineering.

Participants from schools, colleges, and universities present innovative solutions and prototypes addressing real-world challenges. The event typically includes live demonstrations, interactive exhibits, and workshops aimed at fostering a spirit of inquiry and innovation among young minds. Notable features of past festivals have included exhibitions of fire safety and rescue vehicles, artisan demonstrations, and educational workshops on topics like innovative methods of teaching physics.

The festival is open to the public, encouraging community engagement and providing an opportunity for attendees to interact directly with innovators and educators. By promoting hands-on learning and creative problem-solving, the Raman Festival of Innovation plays a significant role in nurturing the next generation of scientists and engineers in India.
University of Toronto Special Award: DEEP Summer Academy Scholarship at IRIS National Science Fair.
2017
The University of Toronto Special Award: DEEP Summer Academy Scholarship is a prestigious recognition presented at the IRIS National Science Fair (Initiative for Research and Innovation in STEM). This award is granted to exceptional student innovators whose projects demonstrate outstanding scientific merit and innovation. Recipients of this award are offered the opportunity to attend the DEEP (Da Vinci Engineering Enrichment Program) Summer Academy at the University of Toronto, a renowned pre-university program that immerses high school students in advanced engineering and science topics through lectures, hands-on projects, and mentorship.

The IRIS National Science Fair serves as a platform for young Indian students from classes 5 to 12 to present their research-based projects in science and engineering. It not only recognizes and rewards outstanding projects but also provides avenues for winners to represent India at international competitions like the International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF). The inclusion of the University of Toronto's DEEP Summer Academy Scholarship as a special award underscores the fair's commitment to fostering global academic collaborations and providing students with opportunities to further their scientific pursuits on an international stage.
NCERT 44th Jawaharlal Nehru National Science, Mathematics and Environment Exhibition CBSE Nominee.
2017
The Jawaharlal Nehru National Science, Mathematics and Environment Exhibition (JNNSMEE) is annual exhibition serves as a platform for students across India to showcase innovative projects in science, mathematics, and environmental studies.

The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) nominates outstanding projects from its affiliated schools to participate in JNNSMEE. Being selected as a CBSE nominee is a significant achievement, reflecting a student's dedication to scientific inquiry and innovation.

The 44th edition of JNNSMEE focused on the theme "Science, Technology and Mathematics for Nation Building", encompassing sub-themes such as health, industry, transport and communication, innovations in renewable resources for sustainable environment, innovations in food production and food security, and mathematical solutions in everyday life .

This experience not only prepares participants for national-level competition but also fosters a deeper understanding of scientific inquiry and innovation.

CBSE Regional Science Fair Top 18 Best Project Award in Transport and Communication Category.
2016
The CBSE Regional Science Exhibition is a competitive platform organized by the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) to foster scientific curiosity, creativity, and innovation among school students. It serves as the first major qualifying round in the journey toward national-level recognition.

Each year, students from CBSE-affiliated schools submit projects based on predefined themes such as health, environment, energy, or transport and communication. These projects are evaluated regionally through exhibitions held across various zones in India. Only a select group of top-performing teams from these regional fairs—those that demonstrate originality, scientific validity, and practical relevance—are shortlisted to advance to the CBSE National Science Exhibition.
