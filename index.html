Namaste, I’m Aman and I’m presently bootstrapping and managing an early-stage startup called Ingenuitive, a private limited company that focuses on affordable Brain-Computer Interfaces and Electric Vehicles for campus commute across colleges in India. While our team works on our visions, we will be launching a pilot platform for students across India to explore hands-on-learning through "Do-It-Yourself Educational Kit on Electronics and Robotics" available online for purchase through our ecommerce website or can be experienced through Atal Tinkering Labs at respective schools across India. In 2022, alongside Ingenuitive, I also launched the GCMS Friend Platform, a website as a service for individuals across the globe who require assistance in progressing with their immigration applications to Canada. We mostly help people understand rejections and advise on meeting the immigration requirements put forward by IRCC, Government of Canada.

In Canada, I serve on the Advisory Board for The Thingery which is a Vancouver based startup building community supported equipment-lending library in a modified storage space. For The Thingery, I am designing the architecture and advising the development of "Integrated Internet, Security, Access Control, and Asset Management System". Simultaneously, I am also contributing to the "Design and Development of Two Factor Biometric Authentication System" for Esaathy Digitals Private Limited which is a startup by Indian Professors for alternative nationwide personal identification in India.

Academically, I am pursuing my undergraduate coursework intending to major in Astrophysics and Computer Science with a focus on Relativity, Cosmology and Dark Energy. Previously, I have worked on the testing and upgradation of the Inner Tracking system for the High-Luminosity Large Hadron Collider operations at the ATLAS Group, University of British Columbia and ProjectALTAIR: Airborne Laser for Telescopic Atmospheric Interference Reduction at VISPA Research Centre, University of Victoria.

Earlier in India, I had the pleasure to build independent projects at Texas Instruments Laboratory, Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology, Nagpur. Awarded with highest distinctions in the most prominent Science Fairs across India, I owe my life to the greatest contributors in our society including my parents, teachers and all my professors. I stay committed to yeilding the highest forms of contributions in scientific research and academia.
